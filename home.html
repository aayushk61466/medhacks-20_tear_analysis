TEAR: Tool for Evaluating & Addressing Readmissions

Hospital readmissions are a burden to our healthcare system, TEAR Analysis can help. Our predictive tool will identify patients most at risk for hospital readmission. With TEAR Analysis, you can ensure these patients receive the assistance they need.

Social Determinants of Health

Education

An individual’s education attainment can drastically influence long-term health prospects through intersecting cognitive, career, and psychosocial pathways. With more education comes healthier choices and accessibility.

Income

Differences in income are correlated with drastic ranges in life expectancy, infant mortality, overall adult and children health, and risks of diabetes, obesity, and heart disease.

Race & Ethnicity

Racial and ethnic groups experience differing incidences of comorbid conditions and life expectancies.

Neighborhood

The safety and accessibility of healthcare resources close to a person’s home can influence their health outcomes.

Relevant Research

What are social determinants of health?

Socioeconomic conditions such as educational attainment, income bracket, race, social support, healthcare accessibility, and neighborhood can drastically shape long-term health outcomes for both individuals and communities. The pattern is almost a stepwise gradient - as social position increases, good health increases.

  • Erica Nelson
Education: Higher education, healthier life

Education and health well-being are intrinsically linked. Through the increase of general knowledge and problem-solving skills, individuals learn to make healthier choices, which often influences their job prospects and earning potential as well. Education also has heavy effects on one’s social networks and perception on one’s own social status.

  • Cindy Kuang
